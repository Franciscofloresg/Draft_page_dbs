Lesson 1 · Wetlands as water filters

Wetlands slow water down, trap sediments and allow plants and microorganisms to break down nutrients and pollutants. Along the Lower Danube, restored floodplains can remove up to 30% of excess nitrogen before it reaches the Black Sea.

Key idea: restoring wetlands is often cheaper, safer and more biodiverse than building grey infrastructure.

Lesson 2 · Restoration techniques

Reconnecting oxbow lakes, removing obsolete dikes, and re-vegetating banks are three core Nature-based Solutions covered in this module.
